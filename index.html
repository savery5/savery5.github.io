An ancient form of cheesecake may have been a popular dish in ancient Greece even prior to Romans' adoption of it with the conquest of Greece. The earliest attested mention of a cheesecake is by the Greek physician Aegimus who wrote a book on the art of making cheesecakes. The earliest extant cheesecake recipes are found in Cato the Elder's De Agri Cultura. Placenta cake is the most like modern cheesecakes: having a crust that is separately prepared and baked.

Chicago style Cheesecake is known for its firm outside and soft and creamy center, which comes from the extra cream cheese that is added to the batter.

Description

If you're a cheesecake lover, my Chicago Style Cheesecake is a cheesecake you definitely need to try. The texture is super soft, moist, and creamy and is guaranteed not to disappoint.

Ingredients

Crust

  1. 1½ cups graham cracker crumbs or shortbread crumbs
  2. 4 tablespoons butter melted

Cheesecake

  • 2½ lbs cream cheese
  • 1½ cups sugar
  • 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 4 large eggs
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon vanilla
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest

Crust

  1. Preheat the oven to 475 degrees F
  2. Combine graham cracker crumbs and melted butter in a mixing bowl. Mix well and place in the bottom of a 10-inch springform pan.
  3. Place the crust in the freezer while you make the cheesecake filling.

Cheesecake

  1. Place the cream cheese into the bowl of a stand mixer and using the paddle attachment, mix the cream cheese on medium-low speed until softened. Scrape down the sides of the bowl and under the paddle often. The process should take 2-3 minutes.
  2. Add the sugar and flour and continue to mix on low until blended (scraping down the sides as needed).
  3. Add the eggs one at a time and mix for about 10 seconds or until just combined.
  4. Add the egg yolks two at a time and mix for about 10 seconds or until just combined.
  5. Add the heavy cream, vanilla, and lemon zest and mix until combined. Don't overmix the batter!
  6. Remove the crust from the freezer and pour in the cheesecake filling.
  7. Carefully place the cheesecake into the preheated water bath. Bake the cheesecake for 12 minutes, then reduce the oven temperature to 350°F.
  8. Let the cheesecake continue to bake until the top of the cheesecake turns golden, which should be about 50 to 60 minutes.
  9. Place the cheesecake on a wire rack to cool.
  10. After cooling, refrigerate the cheesecake for a minimum of 6 hours. Overnight is best.
  11. Serve the cheesecake with fresh berries and homemade whipped cream.
